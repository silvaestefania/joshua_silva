How I Work

Strategy before
a single word.

Good copy isn't written — it's engineered. Every project starts with understanding who we're talking to, what they believe, and what needs to shift for them to act.

01
Audience Research
I map the target reader's awareness level, desires, fears, and the exact language they use to describe their problem.
02
Strategic Brief
Before writing, I define the objective, the one key message, and the behavioral lever — urgency, identity, loss aversion, or social proof — that will drive action.
03
Copy & Structure
Every headline, subhead, and CTA is written to do a specific job. Nothing is decorative. Nothing wastes the reader's attention.
04
Refine to Convert
I cut anything that creates friction, test for clarity against a cold reader, and sharpen until the path from interest to action is frictionless.

The Strategy Behind Each Asset

Facebook Ad
Audience
Long-distance hikers who want an ultralight pack that moves naturally with them
Strategy
Promise ultralight freedom without compromising performance on every mile of the trail
Product Page
Audience
Backpackers frustrated by ultralight packs that sacrifice support or durability
Strategy
Lead with SpinalFrame™ support, 210D Nylon, and breathable mesh — features that eliminate pain on long treks
Abandoned Cart Email
Audience
Hesitant shoppers who left without completing their purchase
Strategy
Benefit-focused body, urgency in the subject line, simple formatting that reduces decision fatigue
Post-Purchase Email
Audience
Recent buyers eager to upgrade their gear
Strategy
Reinforce the “Trailblazer” identity, celebrate their choice, offer a discount that nudges toward gear add-ons
Follow-Up Email
Audience
Buyers settling in with their new pack
Strategy
Practical usage tips + community connection + subtle upsells that feel like added value, not a sales pitch

The Strategy Behind Each Section

Hero / Hook
Objective
Stop the scroll and speak directly to the reader's core desire — not the product, but the experience it enables
Strategy
Lead with an outcome-focused headline that mirrors the language of an outdoor enthusiast, not a product spec sheet
Problem Agitation
Objective
Make the reader feel the cost of their current situation — flimsy gear, ruined trips, wasted money
Strategy
Use specific, relatable scenarios to build emotional tension before introducing the solution
Solution & Features
Objective
Bridge features to real-world benefits the buyer actually cares about
Strategy
Each feature is paired with the outcome it delivers — never leaving the reader to make the connection themselves
Social Proof
Objective
Reduce risk and eliminate doubt at the critical decision point
Strategy
Testimonials framed around transformation, not features — showing what the buyer's life looks like after the purchase
Call to Action
Objective
Convert the committed reader with zero friction at the final step
Strategy
CTA copy reinforces identity ("Join the pack") rather than commanding action — making the click feel like a self-expression, not a purchase
